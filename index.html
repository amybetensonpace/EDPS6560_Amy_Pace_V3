Amy Pace

Amy has spent the last 22 years teaching science to the students and teachers of Utah. She is currently teaching at Mountain Heights Academy; serving as the Science Department Chair, STEM Teams Advisor, and online teacher. She graduated from Southern Utah University in 1998, with a BS in Biology and a Secondary Teaching Certificate. She loves working with her students and fellow teachers. Most recently Amy has worked with both middle school and high school teachers at Mountain Heights Academy to implement the new SEEd standards. Her STEM teams have also received both state and national recognition in several competitions.

Amy has been very involved with the Utah Science Teachers Association in the past, serving three terms as regional representative, and one term as president. Amy has received several prestigious awards during her teaching career; The Presidential Award for Excellence in Math and Science Teaching, The Governorâ€™s Medal for Science and Technology, and was selected to represent the US as part of the TOMODACHI Toshiba Leadership Academy in Tokyo, Japan.

Amy is currently pursuing her Masters in Education as part of the Instructional Design and Educational Technology program. With lofty goals of becoming a leader in digital education, Amy is excited to obtain new skills and further her education in this field. Using these skills, and applying the technology to enhance learning in science and digital curriculum design, will benefit both her school and the teachers she works with. Her ultimate goal is to implement curriculum and training that will benefit all teachers through professional development and digital education.
